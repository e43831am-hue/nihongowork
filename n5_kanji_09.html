🖼️ Where did these kanji come from?

Each kanji began as a picture. Follow the journey from real-world image → ancient script → modern kanji for each character in this set.

年
year
ONネン
KUNとし
Compound Pictograph

年 showed a person carrying a bundle of ripe grain (禾) on their back — the image of harvest time. One harvest equals one full cycle of seasons, so 'harvest' became 'year'.

今
now / present / today
ONコン・キン
KUNいま
Ideograph

今 shows a cover (亼) pressing down, holding something beneath it — the image of something pinned at this exact moment. What is caught under the lid is 'now'.

週
week
ONシュウ
KUN—
Compound Ideograph

週 combines the movement radical 辶 (go around) with 周 (circumference / all around) — the idea of completing one full circuit of the seven days.

度
degree / time / occasion
ONド・ト・タク
KUNたび
Compound Ideograph

度 shows a hand holding a measuring tool under a cliff overhang (广) — measuring a surface. From 'measure' came 'degree', 'temperature', 'angle', and 'occasion' (how many times).

東
east
ONトウ
KUNひがし
Compound Pictograph

東 shows the sun (日) rising behind a tree (木) — dawn seen through a forest. Face the rising sun and you face east. One of the most poetic pictographs in all of kanji.
