Getting Started
1
Open the app
The home screen shows all study modules. Your progress and streak are displayed at the top. No account needed — your progress is saved automatically in your browser.
2
Pick a module
Tap any card on the home screen to open a module. Each card shows the number of items inside. Start with あ Characters if you are a complete beginner.
3
Choose Flashcard or Quiz
Inside each module you will see tabs. Use Flashcard to browse freely, then switch to Quiz to test yourself with 4-choice questions.
4
Review your weak points
Every wrong answer is automatically saved. Tap 📋 Review Quiz or 🧠 Long-term Memory on the home screen to drill your weak items.

How to Use the Quiz
1
Select a level filter
At the top of each module, tap a level button such as N5, N4, or All to narrow the question pool before starting.
2
Switch to Quiz tab
Tap the 🎯 Quiz tab. A question appears showing a word, kanji, or sentence. Read it carefully before choosing your answer.
3
Tap one of the four choices
Choose A, B, C, or D. The correct answer turns green and wrong choices turn red immediately. Feedback explains the right answer.
4
Tap "Next Question"
After seeing the feedback, tap Next Question → to continue. Each question gets its own URL, so you can share or bookmark a specific question.
5
See your score
After 10 questions, your score is shown as a percentage. Tap Try Again to reshuffle and retry, or go back to the home screen.

5 STUDY MODES
⚡ FLASH
Flash Cards
Browse all cards. Tap a card to flip it and see the reading, meaning, and usage example. Use 🔖 to flag cards for later review.
🔤 MEAN
Meaning Quiz
A kanji or term is shown. Choose the correct English meaning from four options. Works for all three card types.
📖 READ
Reading Quiz
A kanji is shown. Choose the correct Japanese reading (on-yomi / kun-yomi) from four options. Kanji cards only.
🔧 EX
Example Quiz
A kanji is shown. Choose the correct usage example (e.g. 電圧, 接地). Tests whether you know how the kanji is used on site. Kanji cards only.
🎯 VOC
Vocab Quiz
An English definition is shown. Choose the correct Japanese term or katakana word. Tests katakana and technical terms.
⚡
Recommended study order: Flash Cards → Meaning Quiz → Reading Quiz → Vocab Quiz. Add Example Quiz last — it is the hardest mode and closest to the real exam format.
🔖
Flag difficult cards using the 🔖 Review button on the flash card. Switch the filter to Flagged to practice only those cards until they feel solid.

1
Select an exam (A–D)
Tap the exam card to see the total question count and time. Then tap Start Exam.
2
Answer within the timer
A 30-minute countdown runs during the exam. Try to keep pace — about 1–2 minutes per question.
3
Review your answers
After finishing, tap Review Answers to see every question with your response highlighted in green (correct) or red (wrong).
🎯
Take all four exams in order. Wrong answers from the mock tests are added to your Review Quiz pool automatically — so every mistake becomes a study card.

Tips & Tricks
🔀
Shuffle often. Tap the 🔀 button to re-randomise the deck. Seeing cards in a different order prevents you from guessing by position.
📊
Check the Dashboard. The Progress Dashboard shows your pass rate per JLPT level and your weakest categories at a glance.
🔁
Reset carefully. The ↺ Reset button restores all cards to the deck. Use it when you want to start fresh, but note it clears your current flagged list too.
🌐
Language switcher. Tap the language pill (🇯🇵 / 🇬🇧 / 🇧🇩) in the top bar to switch the interface between Japanese, English, and Bengali at any time.
📱
Works offline. Once loaded, most features work without an internet connection. Save the page to your home screen for quick access.
